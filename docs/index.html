The Learning Glass Difference

Invented by a Physics Professor

There's nothing more engaging than talking face-to-face. That is why Learning Glass is such a powerful tool for your video presentations. Lightboard technology enables your viewers -- students, colleagues, training participants -- to learn from you as if you were in the same room. There is no new technology for instructors to master, they simply write on the glass as if they were using a whiteboard.

Why It Works

  • The audience can see the presenters’ facial expressions, gestures and body language at all times.
  • The presenter is communicating directly with the audience.
  • Research suggests that gestures and speech are deeply integrated with cognition.
    • Dynamic Drawing Principle – People learn better from a video lecture when the onscreen instructor draws graphics on a board while lecturing rather than referring to already drawn graphics.
    • Gaze Guidance Principle - People learn better from a video lecture when the onscreen instructor shifts gaze between the audience and board while lecturing rather than looking only at the audience or board.
    View The Full Research Here
  • The neon illumination effect creates a strong visual connection with the viewer.
  • Combining the presenter’s personal style (gestures and speech) with the visual effect increases engagement and retention.

How It Works

  • LED lighting on specialised glass creates a transparent white board that illuminates writing with neon markers.
  • Instructor faces the camera and writes normally.
  • Black backdrop and specialist lighting set-up to eliminate reflections.
  • Image is 'flipped' during or in post-production.
  • Pair Learning Glass with camera, iPad or iPhone.
